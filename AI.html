Artificial Intelligence

What is AI?

Artificial intelligence (AI) is the intelligence used by machines or software, as programmed by humans, but can learn to (almost) intuitively learn how to act on their own accordance within a parameter of functions that humans program it.


Vindinium

Class Application: Vindinium

Vindinium is a simple game where the player can code their own AI bot and place them in an arena server to battle against other bots. In this game, bots can reboot health via going to taverns, gain riches via getting gold by claiming mines, and attack others to take mines and gold from other bots in the arena with them. Once killed, other bots take the other's belongings - in this game, its mines and and gold. To reclaim health, players are required to go to a tavern each turn to maintain their health levels, which are capped at 100 points of health. Attacking a mine or other bots to get gold costs 20 health points. Each turn, you lose one health point, so it's imperative to refuel at taverns, which cost 2 gold units to heal 50 health points. Vindinium, in essence, is aimed at teaching beginners how to program simple AI features in a fun and applicable way.


Let's call my bot Dave.
So ... what does Dave do?
Dave uses the first 200 turns to collect gold from mines, and recoup his energy at taverns. After 200 turns, he goes on to attack others with more gold than him, and continues to collect gold and go to taverns, if others around Dave can’t be attacked because they don’t have as much gold.

The three actions I’ve programmed Dave to do are:
1) go to taverns to recoup health,
2) attack others with more gold than him, and
3) collecting gold from mines.

My bot first checks its own life to see whether or not it needs to go to a tavern to heal up – Dave will check to see if his health is above 60 health points or not – if not, he’l go to the tavern. This is done by having a for loop that takes an array of all the taverns, and uses an if statement to change the value of the variable nearTav to whatever the coordinates of the closest tavern is. Then, my AI goes through an if/else statement which first checks for a condition where bot.yourBot.life is less than or equal to 52, and if that is true then it takes the coordinates from the for loop and goes there using bot.findPath.
If Dave’s life isn’t in danger, and it’s been 200 turns, Dave begins his quest! He goes to the nearest free mines and claims its gold, and continues to do so as the turns go on. This is done a lot like how I programmed Dave to do in my tavern function, except in the for loop it takes in an array of all the free mines rather than the taverns.

Then in the same if/else statement, an else ifis added where the condition is that bot.yourBot.life is more than 60 and bot.freeMines.length more than 0, and if that is true then the bot will go towards the nearest free mine. With bot.freeMines.length, if more than 0 does in the conditions is that if there are no more free mines, the bot won't look for free mines.

After this, Dave will look to pillage and steal...so Dave’s programmed to look for others in the game to attack and steal their mines. In this case, Dave is looking for the richest bot to attack.
