Why CS?

It’s as easy as a click of a <-button.>

At least, that’s what I had heard...until I sat down in my first Computer Science class this last September.

But...what shape is that button? Color? Will it glow when you press it? Or hover over it? Where is it placed? What is its tag (and does it match case by case, letter by letter, with any other duplicate buttons)? And the all important question: What does the button exactly...do?

Not so easy after all.

The questions are endless, the processes seemingly convoluted and intertwined. This is computer science, or at least, some of the beginnings I worked with this past year. The mesh of theories and lines, the pairing of scientific procedure and real-life usage. It’s what truly is behind the “like” on that post you just laughed at, the robotic arm that’s manufacturing the newest Tesla, the document you type that dreaded English paper on. Computer science is apart of our daily lives, helping us become more efficiently, bringing us together as a society on the internet.

I mean...there’s an app for that, isn’t there?

Learning what computer science is, and how directly applicable it is in the world has truly opened up my perspective of the world we live in. Coding is complex, it’s a foreign language that mercilessly collapses (or halts altogether) if there’s a single mistake – and for that reason, I have a greater appreciation for all of those who so ardently write the programs, applications, and technologies that we harness today in our daily lives.

My first button I ever coded added up 2+2. Simple, just like a line of code. However, it’s the addition of many, many other lines of code: 2+2+7+3+9, etc. etc. these random addition of lines upon lines of code snippets, they add up – literally in this example, and figuratively when alluding to code – to something so much greater.

Coding is a huge in our lives. The prevalence and possibilities that coding and computer science offer are what inspire me to learn more, and since it’s apart of nearly all aspects of our lives, I hope to harness the power of code to make my work more effective and efficient, whichever field I may end up in, whether it be social media programming, dancing, travelling, or even doing medical research. The opportunities are endless, and coding is extremely prevalent in all that happen in our world, and I can’t wait to learn more and use it more effectively in my own life.
