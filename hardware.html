Keyboard

The keyboard communicates the user's controls and commands through a series of letters, numbers, symbols, and other miscellaneous controls.




Mouse

The mouse navigates the user on the monitor display, as well as activate computer functions.




Monitor

The laptop/computer's screen - that provides the visual outputs from a computer, cable box, video camera, and all other visual displays on the screen, as computed and read by the computer.




Motherboard

The essential foundation of a computer. The motherboard holds together a majority of the key components of a computer, such as the central processing unit (CPU), memory (RAM), and connectors for input and output devices.




CPU

The Central Processing Unit is the "brain" of a computer. It calculates, reads, and processes the codes and functions of computer programs and information to display on the monitor.




RAM

The Random Access Memory is the backbone of what makes a computer. It keeps and records all the data that is computed and created. You may add, delete, and manipulate the information on the RAM. It's like a huge, fancy, flashdrive with about a hundred times the space (it holds up to terrabytes, but currently, RAMs average about 8GB.)
